Add text explaining why ESP8266 was chosen:
"The ESP8266 microcontroller was selected for the ground station receiver to maintain 
architectural consistency with the ESP32-based flight controller. Since the ESP32 features 
integrated Wi-Fi, future implementations can optionally bypass the nRF24L01 radio link 
and establish direct Wi-Fi communication between the drone and ground station. Although 
Wi-Fi significantly decreases the operational range, this forward-compatible design allows 
students or researchers to experiment with Wi-Fi telemetry by simply updating firmware 
on both ends, without hardware modifications."

Add circuit explanation:
"Figure [X] shows the circuit schematic of the ground control station receiver unit. The 
design consists of an ESP8266 microcontroller interfaced with an nRF24L01 2.4GHz radio 
module via SPI communication. The nRF24L01 is connected using standard SPI pins: MOSI, 
MISO, SCK (clock), and CSN (chip select). The CE (Chip Enable) pin controls the radio's 
transmit/receive mode. The nRF24L01 adapter module is powered through the ESP8266's VIN 
pin, which outputs 5V when the ESP8266 is USB-powered; the adapter's onboard regulator 
then provides 3.3V to the radio module."
